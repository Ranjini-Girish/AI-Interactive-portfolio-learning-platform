Implement a turn-based RPG battle simulator in Java that reads six battle configurations from `/app/data/battles/` and an element chart from `/app/data/element_chart.json`, then simulates each battle according to the combat rules documented in `/app/docs/`. The compiled program must be placed at `/app/build/` and the output written to `/app/output/battle_report.json`.

Each battle has heroes and enemies with stats including attack, defense, speed, HP, crit rate, and skills. Entities act in speed-descending order each round, selecting skills by round-based rotation and targeting the lowest-HP opponent. The damage formula applies `floor()` to the attack component and defense component independently before subtraction, then applies elemental multipliers and critical hit bonuses in separate floored stages. Critical hits are determined by a per-battle Linear Congruential Generator seeded from the battle configuration. Status effects like poison, burn, stun, and shields follow precise timing and interaction rules: ticks occur at end-of-round in alphabetical order, burn reduces effective defense, shields absorb damage before HP, and AoE skills deal full damage to every alive target.

Gson is available at `/app/lib/gson.jar` for JSON parsing. Read all specification documents carefully, paying particular attention to the multi-stage floor operations in the damage formula and the deterministic PRNG sequence for critical hits. Output must use sorted keys at all nesting levels with 2-space indentation and a trailing newline.
