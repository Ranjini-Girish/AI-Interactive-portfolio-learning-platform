Build a deterministic Java 21 ledger auditor for a synthetic event-sourced banking trace. Read three JSON inputs from `/app/data/` — `accounts.json`, `events.json`, `snapshots.json` — and a tunables file `/app/data/policy.json`, place your sources under `/app/src/`, compile the entry class to `/app/build/`, and write four JSON reports into `/app/output/`: `account_state.json`, `event_diagnostics.json`, `reconciliation_report.json` and `compliance_summary.json`. The program must accept exactly two positional arguments `<data_dir> <out_dir>` (read inputs from argv[1], write outputs into argv[2], no hardcoded `/app/data` fallback) and must exit non-zero on internal failure. Do not modify anything under `/app/data/`.

Place `.java` sources only under `/app/src/` (no `.cpp`, `.hpp`, or `.py` anywhere under `/app/`). At least 200 lines of authored source under `/app/src/`. Compile with `javac -encoding UTF-8 -cp /opt/gson.jar -d /app/build` from `/app/src/*.java` and run the entry class with `java -cp /app/build:/opt/gson.jar LedgerEventReconAudit <input_dir> <output_dir>`, or ship a runnable script at `/app/build/ledgeraudit` that accepts the same two positional arguments. Exit non-zero on wrong arg count, internal failure, or malformed input. During the run `/app/data/`, `/app/build/`, `/app/src/`, `/app/docs/`, `/app/schemas/`, and `/app/examples/` are immutable; writes belong only in the second-argv output directory.

The supporting reference material is already in the container at `/app/docs/`: `overview.md` describes the simulator at a glance, `field_schemas.md` spells out every field of every input record, `diagnostic_codes.md` is the closed set of diagnostic codes with their evidence keys and severities, and `output_format.md` documents the four output files including their sort orders. The schema files are also enumerated in `/app/docs/schemas/*.schema.json` for programmatic consumption — do not skim past them.

The auditor sorts `events.json` by `seq` ascending before replay, maintains per-account balance, hold list, per-day withdraw total, deposit/withdrawal accumulators, and a reversal-target index, and applies the daily-limit, overdraft-floor, self-transfer, and frozen/closed status gates exactly as specified in `/app/docs/` (the gate predicates, the `E_INSUFFICIENT_FUNDS` `available_cents` formula, and the `E_INVALID_REVERSAL` reason set are normative there). Holds reduce the effective balance for subsequent overdraft checks until released. Reversals skip those gates and apply the inverse legs, subject to the reversal-window and reason rules in `/app/docs/diagnostic_codes.md`. After the trace, expired holds emit `W_HOLD_EXPIRED` and snapshot mismatches emit `N_RECONCILIATION_MISMATCH`; accounts missing from the snapshot map reconcile as `"unsnapshotted"` and emit no diagnostic.

Output JSON is UTF-8, ASCII-only, two-space-indented, with keys lexicographically sorted at every depth and a single trailing newline. `total_deposits_cents` and `total_withdrawals_cents` accumulate the legs that actually moved money (including both legs of a successful transfer and the inverse legs of a successful reversal), not the raw event counts. `n_reversed_events` on an account counts events that were the target of a successful `reverse`, not events the account itself reversed.
